About The Mojave Academy

The Academy structure is different from a normal school day. In a relaxed atmosphere, students will have a unique opportunity to finish their academic classes through an online curriculum. This will allow each student to work at his/her own pace with instructors ready to assist at all times. The Academy can provide ways to finish credits, pass proficiencies, and help students graduate on time. There are teachers, a student success advocate, and a counselor who all work with our students, keeping the class-size one-to-one, and optimizing a student's potential for success.

A student’s success in the Academy is vital to graduation, and our Academy team is highly committed to helping students reach that goal through the Academy pathway.

The Academy's First Graduate!

Audiel Perez

As a sophomore at Legacy High School, Audiel realized that he was headed in a direction he did not want to go. Part of the problem was the friends that he hung around with. Audiel was determined that the best thing for him to do was to separate himself from his friends by going to a different school. This decision was made easier with the support of his girlfriend Angel. She, too, realized that her future was in jeopardy and that a change needed to be made. Both Audiel and Angel enrolled at Mojave last year as juniors and Audiel improved his GPA from 0.89 for his sophomore year to a 3.18 for his junior year. Entering the Mojave Academy this year Audiel needed to complete his regular senior classes as well as make up credits from his freshman and sophomore years. The total of classes that he needed to finish was 10. Because of changes that were made at the beginning of second semester that total became 12. Audiel has worked diligently in the Academy completing on average 2 classes per month. He has been an example to his peers for what could be done when roadblocks are removed and learning is streamlined with an online curriculum. Audiel’s plan now is to attend CSN and pursue a degree in Music Technology. As the Academy staff, we wish you well Audiel and expect that Angel will not be far behind you. We wish you success in all of your future endeavors.

Wade Lybbert

Mr. Lybbert was born in Denver, Colorado to become the third oldest of nine kids. Mr. Lybbert's dad David Lybbert got a degree in microbiology from Colorado State University but did not go into the field of microbiology. After college David tried a number of different jobs that took the family all over the Western United States. Mr. Lybbert changed schools nine times, in three different states before he finally graduated. Mr. Lybbert attended two different high schools. One in Riverside California called Ramona High School with a student population in the thousands. However, he transferred in his senior year to Rye High School with a student population of 201 students.

Mr. Lybbert did not immediately go to college himself. He first served a mission for his church in Alberta, Canada for two years. During his time in Alberta, Mr. Lybbert developed a fondness for Canada – especially the hard working farmers, ranchers, and oil workers he found on the rolling plains of Southern Alberta. It is no surprise that Mr. Lybbert would develop a special connection with these people since his father David Milby Lybbert, his grandfather John Milby Lybbert, and his great grandfather John Isaac Lybbert all worked as blacksmiths and cowboys in the same area specifically Cardston, Alberta. In fact Mr. Lybbert's father, born an American citizen in Utah, had lived his entire life in Alberta until he left to go on a mission when he was 19 to Liverpool, England. Mr. Lybbert still visits Alberta, Canada as often as he can to enjoy the beautiful country there.

After completing his mission and returning to the United States Mr. Lybbert enrolled at Riverside Community College in Riverside, CA. Due to the fact that Mr. Lybbert's high school career was less than stellar he had to work very hard to catch up to where he needed to be for college. It was during this time that he met and married a woman Angela Rannalli and they had their first son in March of 2001. After three years at RCC, Mr. Lybbert received his Associates of Science degree and earned a GPA high enough to transfer to Califronia Polytechnic University - San Luis Obispo to pursue his bachelor's degree. While at Cal Poly San Luis Obispo, Mr. and Mrs. Lybbert had their second son in September of 2003. By March of 2005, Mr. Lybbert had earned his Bachelor of Science degree in Biology and had been accepted to the Southern California College of Optometry in Fullerton California. The Lybbert family moved to Fullerton in August of 2005 to get ready for this next chapter in Mr. Lybbert's education.

During his time at SCCO Mr. Lybbert often tutored high school students and helped his fellow students who did not have a background in biology to get through many of the science classes. He recognized that there was often a disconnect between what the teacher was saying in lecture and what students were understanding. This was the first time that Mr. Lybbert seriously considered teaching at some point in his life. He finished his first year of Optometry School with a 3.4 GPA.

Due to the strain of his educational demands and the conflicting personalities of Mr. Lybbert and his wife Angela they filed for divorce in 2007. The split was harder to take than Mr. Lybbert expected and his schooling suffered. By 2008 Mr. Lybbert's poor performance had resulted in the school dismissing him from the program.

Mr. Lybbert continued to work in various optometry and ophthalmology offices for the next couple of years as a technician doing a lot of the testing he had learned in optometry school. It was during this time that he met and married Lisa Child. Lisa had just finished her masters in education and was looking for a job in elementary education. She was able to find just such a job in Las Vegas, and Mr. Lybbert followed.

After the suggestion of his wife and remembering how he enjoyed teaching his classmates, Mr. Lybbert began a masters program in education. He finished in December of 2011 and was hired by Mojave High School in August 2012. Mr. Lybbert has taught Biology and Chemistry in the last three years here at Mojave and is currently the science teacher in the Mojave Academy. Mojave reminds Mr. Lybbert of his own high school experiences and wants nothing more than to help his students so that they will not have to travel the same hard road he did in order to graduate from college. Mr. Lybbert loves science but has also become very interested in archeology and etymology. He feels that he finds a lot of our own modern problems in ancient societies and hopes to learn from their mistakes.

Tim Wells

Born and raised in Cincinnati, Ohio, Mr. Tim Wells graduated from the University of Cincinnati with his BS in Secondary Education, BA in English, and a Masters in Secondary Education. He earned his leadership for secondary education from Jacksonville State University. He also earned his Specialist in Secondary Education from Georgia State University.

He began his teaching career in Stone Mountain, Georgia. After earning Teacher of the Year among first year teachers, he began working with AP Language courses. Through his success and leadership potential, he began to oversee the AP program. He earned multiple Teacher of the Year awards and STAR teacher awards. With such success in the AP Program, Mr. Wells was promoted to Assistant Principal to open an environmentally themed high school, Arabia Mountain. He led the writing of the curriculum and quickly gained national attention with the EIC model (Environment as an Integrated Curriculum). The school quickly moved to the top three schools in the state of Georgia for test scores and graduation rates. Having received numerous awards from a multitude of agencies, Mr. Wells decided to move to Las Vegas to seek new adventures on improving graduation rates, especially for minority students.

At Mojave High School, he was the Dean of Students during the 2014–2015 school year. During this time he saw an opportunity to create an academy, where it would house students most at risk of not successfully completing high school. Upon promotion to Assistant Principal this current year, the concept of the Academy came to be. Proud to lead such an endeavor, Mr. Wells is excited to see so many students completing missed classes and to witness students feel hope returning to their lives as they can graduate.
